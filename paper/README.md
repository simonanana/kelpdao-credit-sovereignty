# Paper

**Credit Sovereignty in DeFi Lending: Evidence from the KelpDAO Exploit**

> **Abstract.** This paper exploits the April 2026 KelpDAO LayerZero bridge
> exploit as an exogenous shock to identify the causal effect of
> protocol-level vertical integration on DeFi lending resilience. Using a
> four-specification Difference-in-Differences framework on daily TVL panel
> data for Aave V3, Spark, and Morpho (131 days, N = 393 protocol-day
> observations), the preferred conservative estimate finds a 9.7% TVL penalty
> for Aave relative to Spark in the nine post-event days. Pre-event Levene
> variance tests confirm Spark's rate stability is a persistent structural
> property (F = 35.1), and a Baron–Kenny mediation analysis shows 90.7% of
> the differential TVL response is transmitted through the interest-rate
> channel. We attribute this resilience to Spark's Direct Deposit Module — a
> protocol-level credit facility backed by real-world-asset collateral — and
> identify *credit sovereignty* as a measurable resilience factor for DeFi
> lending protocol design.
